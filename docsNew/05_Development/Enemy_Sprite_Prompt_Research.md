**Research Summary:**

## 2. Rocker

**Category:** Insect / Arthropod (Bipedal)
**Classification Status:** RECLASSIFY - currently listed under "Other Arthropod" but is actually a bipedal/anthropomorphic insect. Stands upright on 2 hind legs, uses 2 front limbs as arms holding items.

**Research Summary:**
Anthropomorphic grasshopper (long-horned grasshopper). Stands upright on two large bent hind legs (bipedal stance). Uses two front limbs as arms - one holds a violin with bow, the other holds a large leaf used as a parasol/umbrella. Round chibi-proportioned head with large expressive eyes, two long thin antennae extending from the top of the head. Bright cheerful green coloring all over. Compact rounded body (insect thorax/abdomen). Small, whimsical, non-threatening musician creature. Based on the grasshopper from Aesop's "The Ant and the Grasshopper" fable. Related sprites: Vocal (green, captain variant), Metaller (brown desert variant).

**Research Summary:**

## 10. Mandragora

**Category:** Plant
**Classification Status:** CONFIRMED correct

**Research Summary:**
Squat bulbous fleshy plant creature rooted to the ground. Roughly oval/rounded body, wider than tall, sitting directly on the ground surface. NOT humanoid - no legs (completely immobile, walkSpeed 1000 = effectively stationary), no humanoid arms. Instead has vine-like tentacles/tendrils extending outward from body as attack appendages (long fleshy whip-like vines). Leaf-like or petal-like protrusions around top and sides. Small rudimentary face-like features on front of bulb (simple dot eyes). Muted earthy olive-green to brownish-green body coloring. Pinkish-red/salmon tones on vine tendrils and outer edges. Darker brown accents in crevices. Overall impression: carnivorous parasitic root-mass. Soft body material (flesh, not woody). Found in fields. Slightly unsightly/grotesque appearance.
