## Key Decisions

- **Stationary first** — plants / mushrooms / eggs need only 3 animations (idle / hit / death) and no walk/attack. Rendering these first gets a fast win on raw monster count (13 new entries) with minimal Blender time per model. Higher-effort movers (quadrupeds, flying insects, bipeds) come next.
- **Alias map lives in `build_enemy_list.js`, not in template renames** — renaming `drainiliar.glb` → `drainliar.glb` would break the Tripo3D provenance trail. Cheaper to accept the curated alias table as permanent technical debt (noted in 04-18).
- **Cross-reference doc separate from resume prompt** — `lv1_50_model_matching.md` is the human-oriented audit; `enemy_sprite_session_resume.md` is the machine-reproducible progress snapshot. Both coexist; the former doesn't need to auto-regenerate.
- **Mass import instead of per-batch** — rather than shipping batch 1's 8 atlases one at a time, bundle in the 04-13/14 leftovers and do one bulk UE5 reimport pass. Less context switching between Blender and the editor.
